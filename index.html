PayStructure Pro — Complete Guide

Everything you need to know about salary structuring, tax optimization, and how to use this tool

🎯 What Is PayStructure Pro?

PayStructure Pro is an HR Salary Structuring Optimizer built specifically for Indian payroll, based on Finance Act 2025 provisions applicable from FY 2026-27 (AY 2027-28).

It is not a simple tax calculator. Most tax calculators ask you to enter a fixed salary structure and then tell you the tax. This tool does the opposite — it starts from the total CTC budget and helps you find the smartest way to split it into salary components so the employee pays the least possible tax.

Simple Analogy

Think of it like a pizza. The CTC is the whole pizza. Instead of cutting it randomly, this tool tells you the best way to cut the slices — so that the maximum portion goes into the employee's pocket, and the minimum goes to the tax department.

🧮 What Is "Best Tax Outgo"?

Best Tax Outgo is the lower of your Old Regime tax vs your New Regime tax on the current salary structure — after applying every applicable exemption, deduction, rebate, surcharge, and cess.

Step-by-step computation
1. Gross Salary → subtract exempt components (HRA, Food, LTA…)
2. Old: Std Ded ₹50,000 [Sec 19(1) Sl.2(b)] + Prof Tax ₹2,400 | New: Std Ded ₹75,000 [Sec 19(1) Sl.2(a)], Prof Tax ✗
OLD: subtract 80C + Home Loan + NPS employee → old slabs → 87A → cess
NEW: subtract Employer NPS only → 7 FA2025 slabs → 87A (₹60k, ≤₹12L) → cess
Best Tax Outgo = MIN(Old Regime Tax, New Regime Tax)
Why show the minimum?

Every employee can choose their regime annually. This tool always shows the best-case tax — the regime where you pay less — so you see the true minimum liability achievable with your current salary structure.

🧩 All Salary Components Explained

A salary structure is made up of several components. Some are exempt from tax (reducing your tax burden), some are taxable (fully counted as income), and some are available only in one regime.

Key Principle

Maximise exempt components → minimise Special Allowance (taxable). Every rupee shifted from Special Allowance to an exempt component saves tax.

🎯 How the Optimizer Works — Plain English

Your CTC is fixed. But there are thousands of ways to split the same money across Basic, HRA, PF, NPS, and allowances. Each split produces a different tax bill. This tool tries every valid combination and shows you the one where you pay the least tax.

Simple Analogy

Think of it like packing a suitcase with fixed weight. You can pack the same weight in many ways — but only one arrangement fits everything neatly. The optimizer finds that perfect arrangement for your salary, where every rupee is placed in the most tax-efficient component.

⚙️ The Three Search Dimensions
1️⃣ Basic Salary % — The Anchor
Basic % is tested in 1% steps. Wage Code ON: 50%–60%. Wage Code OFF: 25%–60%.

Basic is the anchor of your salary — changing it moves four things at once:
  • HRA rent exemption ceiling = 50% (metro) or 40% (other) of Basic
  • PF deductions — employee and employer = 12% each of Basic
  • Gratuity — employer saves 4.81% of Basic for you every year
  • NPS employer deduction cap = up to 14% of Basic
A ₹1 change in Basic can save ₹3 in tax because of how HRA works. The optimizer finds this sweet spot.
2️⃣ Employer NPS % — The Cross-Regime Saver
Employer NPS is tested from 0% to 14% of Basic in 1% steps.

This is the only tax saving that works in both Old and New Regime. Your employer puts money into your NPS account — it reduces your taxable income in both regimes. Maximum 14% of Basic (Finance Act 2025 raised this from 10%). The optimizer finds the exact % where tax saving beats the take-home reduction — this point is different for every person.
3️⃣ Allowances — ON or OFF for Each
Six allowances are each tested ON or OFF — LTA, Meal Coupons, Mobile, Education, Hostel, DA. That gives 64 combinations (2⁶).

Each allowance is only worth including if it saves more tax than it costs to administer. LTA saves tax only in Old Regime. In New Regime, most allowances are fully taxable — so the optimizer often recommends fewer allowances and more Employer NPS instead.

💡 Why This Matters — Two Key Insights
The Non-Linear HRA Cliff

The HRA exemption formula — Minimum of: Actual HRA  ·  Rent minus 10% of Basic  ·  50%/40% of Basic — creates inflection points. At certain Basic values, the binding constraint switches from one formula to another. Below that point, increasing Basic adds HRA exemption rupee-for-rupee. Above it, there is no further benefit. This inflection point is different for every employee's rent amount and CTC — exhaustive search is the only reliable way to find it.

The NPS Crossover Point

Employer NPS moves money from Special Allowance (taxable) to NPS (deductible in both regimes). But it also reduces current liquid cash. The optimizer finds the exact NPS% where the tax saving equals the contribution cost — the crossover point varies by income level and cannot be determined manually without computing dozens of scenarios.

🆕 What Changed in Finance Act 2025?
Major Changes — Finance Act 2025 (Effective FY 2026-27)

1. New Regime: Zero tax up to ₹12L — ITA 2025 Sec 156(2) rebate raised to ₹60,000 (from ₹25,000 under old Sec 87A). Anyone with taxable income ≤ ₹12,00,000 pays zero tax in New Regime.

2. New Regime slabs restructured — 7-slab structure replacing the older 6-slab structure. The 10% slab now covers ₹8–12L (earlier it was 5%).

3. Employer NPS: 14% for private sector — ITA 2025 Sec 124(2) cap raised to 14% of Basic+DA (Old Regime: 10% via Sec 124(1)(b)). Available in BOTH regimes — the only Chapter VIII deduction in New Regime.

4. New Regime is now the default — Employees must explicitly opt for Old Regime. If no declaration is made, New Regime applies.

🤔 When to Choose Which?
✅ Choose Old Regime if…
You pay substantial rent (HRA exemption is high), you have a home loan with significant interest (₹1.5–2L), you max out 80C investments (PPF, ELSS, LIC), and the total of these deductions is large enough to bring taxable income below what new slabs offer.
✅ Choose New Regime if…
Your taxable income is ≤ ₹12L (zero tax after rebate), you don't have significant exemptions/deductions, you prefer simplicity, or your income is very high (above ₹24L) where the lower new slab rates save more than the available deductions.

💡 Key Concepts You Must Know
📦 What Is CTC vs Gross Salary vs In-Hand?
CTC (Cost to Company) = Everything the employer pays including Employer PF, Gratuity provision, Employer NPS. This is the total budget.

Gross Salary = All components that appear in the payslip (Basic + HRA + Allowances). Employer PF and Gratuity are NOT in gross salary.

In-Hand / Take-Home = Gross Salary minus Employee PF deduction, Professional Tax, and Income Tax TDS. This is what hits the bank account every month.
🔢 Why Does Basic Salary % Matter So Much?
Basic is the anchor that drives multiple calculations simultaneously:
• HRA exemption ceiling = 50%/40% of Basic
• Employer + Employee PF = 12% + 12% of Basic each
• Gratuity = 4.81% of Basic
• Employer NPS = up to 14% of Basic

A higher Basic means higher PF deductions but higher HRA exemption ceiling. Lower Basic means more cash-in-hand but less retirement savings. The optimal range is 35–45% of CTC for most employees under the old structure. Under Code on Wages 2019 (when notified), Basic must be ≥50% of total remuneration — making the old 35% Basic structures non-compliant. Enable Wage Code mode in Step 1 to model compliant structures.
📊 How Is HRA Exemption Calculated?
HRA exemption = Minimum of these 3 amounts:
1. Actual HRA received from employer
2. Actual rent paid MINUS 10% of Basic Salary
3. 50% of Basic (Metro) or 40% of Basic (Non-Metro)

Example: Basic ₹4,80,000/yr | HRA ₹2,40,000/yr | Rent ₹20,000/month (₹2,40,000/yr) | Metro
(a) ₹2,40,000 | (b) ₹2,40,000 − ₹48,000 = ₹1,92,000 | (c) ₹2,40,000
Exempt = ₹1,92,000/year
💼 What Is Professional Tax?
Professional Tax is a state-level tax on employment income (state-wise slab, usually up to ₹2,500/year). In this tool, you can enter your actual annual PT value from payroll records. It is deductible only in Old Regime and blocked in New Regime.
🏦 What Happens with PF If Basic Exceeds ₹15,000/Month?
The statutory EPF wage ceiling is ₹15,000/month (₹1,80,000/year). For statutory compliance, both employer and employee must contribute 12% of at least ₹15,000. If Basic is higher, voluntary PF on the excess is optional. This tool caps PF at ₹1,80,000 wage for the statutory minimum calculation.

⚡ Pro Tips
Top Tax-Saving Moves

1. Employer NPS — Add 14% of Basic as Employer NPS. It saves tax in BOTH regimes. Under Finance Act 2025, this is the single biggest untapped opportunity for private sector employees.

2. Meal Coupons (Old Regime) — Always include ₹26,400 if using Old Regime. Exempt under Rule 3(7)(iii). In New Regime, Sec 202(2)(c) makes them fully taxable — redirect that budget to Employer NPS instead.

3. Optimise Basic — For New Regime employees, lower Basic = lower PF outflow = more in-hand. For Old Regime with high rent, higher Basic = higher HRA exemption ceiling.

5. Mobile/Internet allowance — Even ₹12,000/year (₹1,000/month) saves ₹3,600 in tax for a 30% bracket employee. Keep bills.

🏛️ Code on Wages 2019 — The Biggest Change in Indian Labour Law Since 1948

The Code on Wages 2019 consolidates four labour laws — Minimum Wages Act 1948, Payment of Wages Act 1936, Payment of Bonus Act 1965, and Equal Remuneration Act 1976. Its redefinition of "wages" is the single biggest structural change to Indian salary design in decades, directly impacting Income Tax calculations, PF, ESIC, Gratuity, and Bonus.

Four Acts consolidated into one Code
🔴 Minimum Wages Act, 1948 → Code on Wages 2019
🔴 Payment of Wages Act, 1936 → Code on Wages 2019
🔴 Payment of Bonus Act, 1965 → Code on Wages 2019
🔴 Equal Remuneration Act, 1976 → Code on Wages 2019
⚡ Status: Passed. Central notification pending. Many states & large employers already complying voluntarily.
📐 Sec 2(88) — The New Definition of "Wages"

This is the central provision. The Code defines "wages" with an inclusion + exclusion approach. What's in defines your PF base, ESIC base, Gratuity base, Bonus base, and minimum wage compliance. And crucially — what's in determines the 50% rule.

✅ INCLUDED in "Wages" [Sec 2(88)(a)]
  • Basic Salary
  • Dearness Allowance (DA)
  • Retaining Allowance (if any)
  • Any other allowance payable universally to all employees in a category
❌ EXCLUDED from "Wages" [Sec 2(88)(b)]
  • House Rent Allowance (HRA)
  • Overtime allowance
  • Conveyance / travel allowance
  • Any special allowance ≤50% of wages
  • Employer contributions to PF/ESIC/pension
  • Gratuity provision
  • Travel concession (LTA)
  • Bonus, commission on sales
  • Retrenchment/VRS compensation
⚡ The Critical "50% Cap" Clause

If total excluded components (HRA + allowances + perquisites) exceed 50% of total wages, the excess automatically becomes part of "wages." This prevents employers from structuring salaries as 10% Basic + 90% allowances. The Code forces a genuine Basic-heavy structure.

🏦 PF Deep Dive — How Wage Code Changes Retirement Math
Two PF Scenarios — Same CTC of ₹12L/year
❌ Old Structure (Basic 30%)
Basic = ₹3,60,000/yr
PF wage = Min(₹3,60,000, ₹1,80,000) = ₹1,80,000
Employee PF = ₹21,600/yr
Employer PF = ₹21,600/yr
Annual PF contribution = ₹43,200/yr
30yr corpus (at 8%) = ~₹52 lakhs
✅ Wage Code (Basic 52%)
Basic = ₹5,52,000/yr (≥50% after excl.)
PF wage = ₹5,52,000 (actual, no cap if employer chose)
Employee PF = ₹66,240/yr
Employer PF = ₹66,240/yr
Annual PF contribution = ₹1,32,480/yr
30yr corpus (at 8%) = ~₹1.61 crores

⚡ The difference is staggering — ₹1.09 crore more retirement corpus purely from the Wage Code's 50% Basic rule, for the same CTC. The cost: slightly lower monthly take-home (about ₹3,700/month less), higher tax savings via larger NPS cap and HRA ceiling.

📈 Employer NPS — The Wage Code Multiplier Effect

When Wage Code raises Basic, it also raises the Employer NPS cap under ITA 2025 Sec 124(2). This creates a compounding tax benefit.

NPS Cap Comparison — CTC ₹12L
Old (Basic 30%): 14% × ₹3.6L = ₹50,400 NPS cap/yr
New Code (Basic 52%): 14% × ₹5.52L = ₹77,280 NPS cap/yr
Extra NPS deduction available: ₹26,880/yr
Tax saving at 20% rate: ₹5,376/yr more saved

🗺️ Transition Planning — What Employers Must Do
Step 1 — Audit Current Structures
Identify all employees where Basic < 50% of total remuneration. Calculate PF shortfall from date of notification. Prepare cost impact analysis — larger PF = larger employer PF expense in P&L.
Step 2 — Restructure Without Salary Cuts
The usual approach is Cost-Neutral Restructuring: raise Basic to 50%, reduce Special Allowance by same amount. CTC stays same. Employee take-home drops slightly (more PF outflow) but gross tax remains similar. Net benefit: higher retirement corpus, higher HRA exemption ceiling (Old Regime), higher NPS cap (both regimes).
Step 3 — Recalibrate Tax Planning
With higher Basic, use PayStructure Pro's Wage Code mode to find the new optimum. The optimizer automatically enforces ≥50% Basic and finds the correct NPS%, HRA, and allowance combination that minimises tax within the legal structure.
Step 4 — Communicate to Employees
Employees will see: (a) slightly lower monthly take-home due to higher PF deduction, (b) higher long-term retirement corpus, (c) potentially lower tax if HRA/NPS benefits increase. The net impact depends on the employee's tax regime and rent situation — use this tool to model it per employee.
